This is a report on VFTS 243, a massive X-ray faint binary in the
LMC. It consists of an O-type star of 25 M_sun and an unseen companion
of >9 M_sun with orbital period 10.4 d. The unseen companion is
implied to be a black hole. When this paper was published, VFTS 243
was the only unambiguously known BH outside the Milky Way, allowing
observations to probe previously unobserved physics, which influences
the predicted rates of GW detections and overall properties of
core-collapse supernovae throughout the Universe.

One discovery made from VFTS 243 is its nearly-circular orbit, which
suggests that the system was not impacted by a significant supernova
kick. Also, the system is theorized to become a binary BH system
within 5 Myr, with a merger on the order of 10 - 100 Gyr.

Note that unlike the dozens of examples of refuted X-ray quiescent BH
binaries that preceded this discovery, VFTS 243 exhibits an ordinary
O-type spectrum and its characteristics explicitly disallow the
stripped low mass stars that had caused the previous false alarms.

Special note: This paper includes a 25 M_sun stellar model computed
with MESA to find the synchronization and circulartization timescales
run by Pablo Marchant. This is the code I have recreated and am
currently varying the mass-loss framework for, as I wrote about
yesterday. (Note that the results are included in Supplementary Figure
